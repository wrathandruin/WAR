# Wrath and Ruin - Lore and Visual Theme Guide

## Purpose

This document adapts the strongest lore material from the older `Wrath-and-Ruin` repo into a visual-theme guide for the current `WAR` production.

It exists so the art team is not inventing tone, faction identity, signage language, costume cues, and environmental logic from milestone mechanics alone.

## Core Setting Truths

Wrath and Ruin is a human hard-science-fiction setting.

The baseline world truth is:

- humanity has spread through the inner Solar System
- there is no faster-than-light travel
- travel is slow, expensive, and dangerous
- orbital corridors, docking infrastructure, relay systems, cargo movement, maintenance, and life support are central to daily life
- Earth-origin institutions still matter, but frontier identities are stronger farther out
- scarcity, wear, maintenance, and patchwork survival are normal
- rumors of buried anomalies and hidden history exist, but that mystery should not dominate everyday visual language

Art implication:

The world should look built, repaired, repurposed, inspected, patched, and kept running under pressure.

It should not look magical, alien, ceremonial, or sleek for its own sake.

## The Visual Mood

The visual mood should be:

- industrial
- grounded
- pressure-aware
- maintenance-heavy
- cargo-driven
- utilitarian
- weathered
- inhabited
- readable

The visual mood should not be:

- glossy luxury sci-fi
- space opera grandeur
- neon cyberpunk spectacle
- sterile white-lab futurism
- junk-heap chaos with no institutional order
- fantasy-tech ornament

## What Makes The Setting Distinct

The setting should feel like a world where these systems matter:

- docking collars
- cargo staging
- pressure doors and airlocks
- hazard markings
- maintenance access
- route terminals
- reactor and power systems
- relay infrastructure
- shipboard discipline
- worn habitation modules

Every important location should communicate function before decoration.

## Political And Institutional Identity

These identities should inform signage, markings, uniform trims, prop logic, and environment tone.

### UNEA

Role:

- formal authority
- treaty enforcement
- traffic regulation
- administration

Visual cues:

- clean but not luxurious institutional surfaces
- disciplined markings
- restrained blues, greys, off-whites
- formal signage language
- legible numbering and hazard compliance systems

### Martian Commonwealth

Role:

- heavy industry
- spacecraft manufacturing
- propulsion engineering

Visual cues:

- more rugged industrial confidence
- deeper reds, rusts, darker metals, heat-stained surfaces
- practical engineering identity
- welded reinforcement and field modifications that still feel competent

### Belt Cooperative

Role:

- unions
- guilds
- contractors
- station labor

Visual cues:

- practical modular gear
- patched workwear
- cargo tags, salvage markings, union-coded identifiers
- denser wear, mixed equipment provenance, stronger improvisation

### Jovian Development Authority

Role:

- infrastructure under pressure
- fuel, ice, and protection systems

Visual cues:

- heavy insulation
- sealed piping
- colder palettes
- thicker environmental protection hardware
- bulk systems designed to endure harsh conditions

### Solar Research Council

Role:

- science
- instrumentation
- observation

Visual cues:

- cleaner instrument surfaces
- analytical, labeled equipment
- subtle teal/cyan data accents
- less grime than industrial zones, but still practical and lived-in

## Costume And Equipment Language

Characters should look human and practical first.

Friendly silhouettes should be differentiated by:

- workwear
- tool rigs
- protective gear
- service markings
- function-specific helmets or headsets

Hostile silhouettes should be differentiated by:

- scavenged armor
- asymmetry
- rougher combinations of civilian and tactical kit
- damaged or improvised field gear

Do not solve faction identity primarily through wild color blocking.
Solve it through silhouette, markings, gear logic, and material use.

## Signage And Typography Guidance

Signage should feel operational, not decorative.

Good signage:

- route identifiers
- dock numbering
- hazard labels
- cargo IDs
- system labels
- direction arrows
- quarantine and decon warnings
- relay and docking identifiers

Bad signage:

- over-stylized sci-fi glyphs
- fantasy symbols
- decorative logos with no operational meaning

## The MUD Identity In Visual Form

Wrath and Ruin is not only a top-down tactics game.
It must still feel like a graphical MUD.

That means the art must leave space for:

- room and location titles
- descriptive text
- inspectable objects
- mission and event logs
- social and speech surfaces
- prompt and vitals readability

Visual design should support text-first identity rather than compete with it.

## What To Avoid

Avoid:

- alien ruins as everyday set dressing
- luminous fantasy relic shapes
- glossy clean corridors everywhere
- wildly exaggerated cyberpunk color
- side-on wall language
- decorative clutter that blocks tactical reading
- “cool junk” with no function

## Working Rule

If an asset idea does not reinforce:

- function
- infrastructure
- scarcity
- repair
- faction identity
- room identity
- or text-supported world atmosphere

then it is probably the wrong priority for this project.
